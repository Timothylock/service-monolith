Being A UofT Student During The Strike

  • 11 Mar 2015
  • Timothy Lock
  • Blog
  • Leave a comment

Being at student at UofT (which as we know the TAs are on strike), I was heartbroken to see my instructor (on strike) sitting outside our class while a replacement instructor came in to teach. Don’t get me wrong, having a replacement is not what I am upset about (that’s the least the university can do for us students).

What upset me is that she was sitting outside not holding a picket sign or anything. She wasn’t even even holding pamphlets or anything to promote CUPE 3902 or anything. She was just sitting there out of her own time to answer any questions that we had about the strike. Whether this was a move by the union to make us students side with them or not, the fact that she was sitting out there in the cold answering our questions is a hell of a lot more than the university administration has been doing (They still keep us in the dark).

In fact, I saw my TA outside of class and I told her how I was worried about the course and grades and stepping on the boundary, my TA gave me tips to studying, what I should focus on and all that despite them not supposed to be talking about course work during their strike. Its nice to see that the TAs aren’t only pretending to care about us (which in my opinion is all the university has been doing). Or maybe they are… but they are still hiding it a lot better than the university (lets face it. All the “good” benefits that the university gives us students is because UTSU fought for them).

I may agree that some of the union’s requests are unreasonable and that the university has to see the bigger picture, manage funding, etc and that they have their reasons; but clearly the university has to tackle this issue rather than waiting it out. Using students as a bargaining chip is just really disappointing.
