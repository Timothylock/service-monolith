MHacks V (2015)

About: Students spend 36 hours building anything they can imagine, and take breaks to interact with technology visionaries, founders, and find new mentors. They meet with the hottest, most sought-after companies in the world who vie for hackers’ attention. For MHacks, students give up a weekend of partying to invest a weekend in their personal growth by learning something new, and creating solutions to problems they see in the world.
Project: http://challengepost.com/software/dumpaware
Ranking: Apple sponsor prize

MLH Local Hack Day (2014)

About: A 12 Hour global event celebrating the Hacker community
Project: https://github.com/Timothylock/league-carnage-notifier-Raspberry-Pi

 

Hack The North (2014)

About: This is a 36 hour hackathon where like all hackathons: you come up with an idea and you program/build it. Sleep optional.
Project: http://challengepost.com/software/pebblettc 

 

Waterloo Canadian Computing Competition (2013)

About: This is a 3 hour contest composed of 5 questions (either at the Junior or Senior Level) where students were to create a program that could complete the objective. The code is then uploaded and test cases are ran against it.
Ranking: Winner (Perfect Score)

UOIT Engineering Robotics Competition (2013)

About: Teams each built a robot to run the robocross track. The objective was to run 3 laps the fastest while overcoming obstacles and eliminating other robots on the track.
Ranking: First Place (St. Augustine CHS Team A)
