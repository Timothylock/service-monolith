Continue Reading →]]> At last, I finally came home from university and I got a chance to look closely at the TVpad. Ever since my parents got it, I was curious on what it ran on (let’s face it, a lot of knockoff Chinese media players run android). The website said it was android 4.4 and at that point, I made it my mission to be able to install my own apk files on it.

It wasn’t an easy task. The developers made quite the effort to hide the default system UI from the user. At first, I plugged in a mouse and low and behold. Dragging the cursor down from the top of the screen produced the android notification center. From there, I was able to get into the settings screen and see all the goodies. Suprisingly, it seems as if the developers shrunk the top bar into a few pixels height. Clever trick! Unfortunately, I can’t install apks from that screen.

So at this point, I was a bit disappointed. A good lead but it got me nowhere. Then I opened up TVpad’s internet browser and downloaded ES File Manager apk. Just like that, it installed. Now I was able to look into the system and “unlock” the default apps (dialer, browser, etc). On addition to that, I was able to extract all of their own apps (their TV apps). Going to see if it can run on my other android stick!

Continue Reading →]]> Being at student at UofT (which as we know the TAs are on strike), I was heartbroken to see my instructor (on strike) sitting outside our class while a replacement instructor came in to teach. Don’t get me wrong, having a replacement is not what I am upset about (that’s the least the university can do for us students).

What upset me is that she was sitting outside not holding a picket sign or anything. She wasn’t even even holding pamphlets or anything to promote CUPE 3902 or anything. She was just sitting there out of her own time to answer any questions that we had about the strike. Whether this was a move by the union to make us students side with them or not, the fact that she was sitting out there in the cold answering our questions is a hell of a lot more than the university administration has been doing (They still keep us in the dark).

In fact, I saw my TA outside of class and I told her how I was worried about the course and grades and stepping on the boundary, my TA gave me tips to studying, what I should focus on and all that despite them not supposed to be talking about course work during their strike. Its nice to see that the TAs aren’t only pretending to care about us (which in my opinion is all the university has been doing). Or maybe they are… but they are still hiding it a lot better than the university (lets face it. All the “good” benefits that the university gives us students is because UTSU fought for them).

I may agree that some of the union’s requests are unreasonable and that the university has to see the bigger picture, manage funding, etc and that they have their reasons; but clearly the university has to tackle this issue rather than waiting it out. Using students as a bargaining chip is just really disappointing.
